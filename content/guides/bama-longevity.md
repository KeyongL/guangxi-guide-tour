Bama 巴马 is a rural county in Hechi 河池, in the karst hills of northwest Guangxi. It is famous inside China as a "longevity village" 长寿乡 — a place with an unusually high count of very old residents that has grown into a domestic wellness-tourism town. For a foreign traveller on the coastal Beihai–Vietnam route, Bama 巴马 is a real detour: roughly a 3.5-hour drive inland from Nanning 南宁, and there is nothing else major nearby. It is inside the 240-hour transit visa-free zone, so unlike Detian or Leye there is no visa-zone restriction on visiting. Go if you want green karst valleys, cave walks, and a look at a very Chinese style of health tourism. Skip it if you are short on time or expect miracle air — the scenery is nice, but it is a long way for it.

## The 'longevity village' 长寿乡, minus the hype

Bama 巴马 does have a documented cluster of centenarians. Reporting puts the county population near 240,000 with more than 80 residents over 100 (not yet verified against a recent census). That statistic is real enough to have made the place a domestic brand.

What comes next is marketing, so read it plainly. Local tourism leans hard on claims about "negative oxygen ions" in the cave air, "small-molecule" alkaline river water, and selenium-rich soil. You will see these repeated as fact on tour pages and hotel signs. Treat them as sales copy, not medicine. Scientists still debate why Bama 巴马 has so many old people — diet, genetics, an active rural life, and clean air are all plausible, and none of them is a cure you can buy. Do not come expecting your health to change. Come for the landscape and the curiosity of it.

The town also draws long-stay domestic "health migrants" — retirees who rent rooms for months to breathe the air and soak in the river. It is a genuine social phenomenon and interesting to see, even if you do not buy the theory behind it.

## What's actually there: caves and a river

The main sights are natural and close together:

- **Baimo Cave 百魔洞** — a large karst cave the Panyang River 盘阳河 runs through. It is the headline attraction; reporting cites roughly two million visitors a year (not yet verified). Expect crowds and people meditating in the cave mouth for the "ion" air.
- **Panyang River 盘阳河 valley** — a calm green river winding through karst peaks and farm villages. The valley walk and the riverside are the nicest part of a visit.
- **Karst scenery and Yao 瑶族 villages** — terraced fields, limestone hills, and small minority settlements around the valley.

Entrance fees exist for the caves and scenic areas but change often — confirm current prices at the gate (not yet verified).

## Getting there (and whether it's worth it)

There is no airport or train station in Bama 巴马 itself. Most people come by road from Nanning 南宁 or via Hechi 河池:

- **From Nanning 南宁**: direct long-distance buses run to Bama, around 4 hours; one source lists departures at 12:20, 15:20, and 17:30 and a fare near ¥130 (not yet verified — check current schedules). Driving is about 3.5 hours.
- **Via Hechi 河池**: Nanning 南宁 to Hechi 河池 is about a 2-hour drive, and Hechi has road and rail links, but Bama sits off the main line and still needs a road connection (not yet verified).

Honest verdict: for most Western travellers doing the coastal Beihai–Weizhou–Vietnam loop, Bama 巴马 is out of the way. It only makes sense if you are already heading inland and want a couple of slow days in the karst. It is not a quick side trip.

## Yao 瑶族 culture

Bama 巴马 is a Yao 瑶族 autonomous county, and the Yao are the main local minority. You will see Yao villages, dress, and food around the Panyang River 盘阳河 valley. Some tourism is staged for visitors, so expect a mix of everyday village life and performance. Treat homes and residents — including the elderly the tours point at — as people, not exhibits.

## Practical notes

- **Best base**: villages around Baimo Cave 百魔洞 and the Panyang River 盘阳河 have guesthouses aimed at long-stay domestic visitors.
- **Pace**: this is a slow, rural destination. Two nights is plenty for the caves and river.
- **Language**: little English. Have your destinations written in Chinese and use a translation app.
- **Cash**: bring some; mobile pay dominates but rural vendors and bus fares can be easier with cash. See our foreign-card WeChat/Alipay guide.
- **Health claims**: enjoy the air and water if you like, but do not treat anything here as medical treatment.
