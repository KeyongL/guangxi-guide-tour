Start with the rule that trips most people: China's 240-hour (10-day) transit
visa-free policy does **not** support entering by land. Its entry points are
limited to designated airports and cruise ports. So you cannot walk across the
border from Vietnam on a transit-visa-free basis, even though the coast you are
heading for (Beihai 北海, Fangchenggang 防城港) sits inside the transit zone.

That leaves two legal ways to enter Guangxi overland from Vietnam:

1. You hold a passport in China's **30-day unilateral visa-free program** (roughly
   48 countries — most of Europe, Australia, New Zealand and others). The United
   States is **not** on this list.
2. You hold a **valid Chinese visa** of any type that permits entry.

If neither applies to you, get a Chinese visa before you travel. Do not plan to
"sort it at the border." Everything below assumes you already qualify, and that
you cross at the official Dongxing 东兴 land port and clear immigration normally.

## Can you actually do this? Check your passport first

The unilateral visa-free list changes. Confirm your nationality against the
current National Immigration Administration (NIA) list before booking anything
(check current NIA list). Two people on the same trip can have different rights
here: an Australian may enter visa-free for 30 days while an American on the same
tour needs a visa in advance.

Whichever category you fall into, you enter through the immigration hall at
Dongxing like any other traveller: passport, honest answers about your plans, and
a stamp. Nothing on this page describes any other way across.

## Route 1: Móng Cái to Dongxing 东兴

This is the short, popular crossing. The Vietnamese border town of Móng Cái sits
directly across the Beilun River 北仑河 from Dongxing 东兴 in Guangxi. You exit
Vietnam, ride the shuttle across the road bridge — there is no footpath, so you
do not walk it — and clear Chinese immigration on the Dongxing side.

Once stamped in, Dongxing puts you about an hour from the Fangchenggang 防城港
coast and within reach of Beihai 北海 and its ferries to Weizhou 涠洲岛.

- Border-crossing hours: 08:00–21:00 China time (07:00–20:00 Vietnam time). There
  is no blanket holiday closure — the port stayed open through Chinese New Year
  2026 on adjusted hours, though no holiday-specific timetable is published.
- Which bridge you cross: ordinary passport holders use the **Bắc Luân II bridge**,
  a vehicle bridge with no footpath; a shuttle carries foot passengers between the
  two immigration halls. The older Bắc Luân I bridge is now restricted to
  border-resident permits, multi-entry permits, and visa-on-arrival travellers.
- Onward bus, Dongxing → Beihai: about 208 km, departures every 30–90 minutes
  (roughly 06:20–18:50, last around 20:20, 12–16 runs a day), ¥35 standard or ¥68
  premium. Journey time is (not yet verified) — one source claims 1.5 h, which is
  not credible for the distance; budget 2.5–3.5 h.

Do not photograph the border installations, checkpoints, or any military area.
Put the camera away until you are well past immigration.

## Route 2: Hà Nội to Nanning 南宁

If you are starting from Hà Nội and want to reach the provincial capital, there
are two overland options.

**Cross-border bus.** Direct coaches run between Hà Nội and Nanning 南宁, crossing
at the Youyiguan / Friendship Pass corridor near Pingxiang 凭祥. The bus stops at
the border so every passenger clears immigration on foot, then reboards.

- Bus: operated by **Guangxi Yunde Transport Group 广西运德汽车运输集团**. Roughly
  10 departures a day from Nanning, first around 07:40, about 8 hours to the
  border. Fare is around ¥170 — a best estimate, not an official published price
  (not yet verified).

**Rail.** Train **T8701/MR2** runs daily from Nanning 南宁 to Hà Nội (Gia Lâm),
leaving 18:05 and arriving 06:30 the next morning — about 12 h 25 min. The return,
**T8702/MR1**, leaves 22:20 and arrives 10:06. There is one class only, soft
sleeper, at ¥215 (about $30). It stops at the border for immigration formalities
overnight.

- Buy at the ticket counter with your passport. There is no online sale.
- You need a **paper visa** in hand — Dong Dang does not accept e-visas.
- The service has been interrupted before: it stopped in February 2020 for the
  pandemic and only resumed on 25 May 2025. It has run continuously since, and was
  still running as of April 2026 — but confirm it is operating on your dates before
  you build a plan around it.

For both options, confirm that your visa or visa-free status is valid **before**
you reach the border. Staff will not issue one there.

## What to see once you're in

Dongxing 东兴 and the Fangchenggang 防城港 coast give you mangroves and quiet
beaches. Push on to Beihai 北海 for the Old Town and the ferry to Weizhou 涠洲岛,
a volcanic island off the coast. Nanning 南宁 is the transport hub if you plan to
fly out or continue by high-speed rail into the rest of Guangxi.

## Confirm before you travel

- Your nationality's current visa-free status, or that your Chinese visa is valid
  and permits land entry (check current NIA list).
- That the specific border crossing you plan to use is open to foreign nationals
  on your dates — not every land port accepts every passport.
- Live schedules and fares for any bus or train above. Treat every "(not yet
  verified)" figure here as a prompt to check, not a booking detail.
- Your onward plan inside China: where you sleep the first night, and how you
  eventually leave.

Cross only at the official Dongxing land port, clear immigration in the normal
way, and keep your documents in order. That is the whole route.
