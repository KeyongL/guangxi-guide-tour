Yes. A foreign visitor can pay with WeChat 微信 and Alipay 支付宝 in China today, without a Chinese bank account. Both apps now let you bind an overseas card — Visa, Mastercard, and several other networks — and pay by scanning QR codes. You verify your passport once, add your card, and you are ready. Do the setup at home before you fly. Also carry some cash, because a few small vendors still take domestic-only QR codes.

## Which app: Alipay 支付宝 or WeChat 微信?

Both work. If you only install one, install Alipay 支付宝. Its foreign-card flow has been open longer and its English menus are cleaner. WeChat 微信 is worth adding too, because it doubles as your messaging and contact app in China, and some merchants show only one of the two QR codes.

Practical split: set up Alipay 支付宝 as your main wallet, and add WeChat 微信 as backup. Between the two you will cover almost every shop, taxi, and restaurant.

One limit to know: on a foreign card, both apps block peer-to-peer transfers and red packets (发红包). You can pay merchants, but you cannot send money to another person's wallet. Plan around that.

## Set it up before you fly

Do all of this on home wifi, days before departure. Setup needs a working phone number for the verification code and a clear photo of your passport.

1. Download Alipay 支付宝 and WeChat 微信 from your normal app store.
2. Register with your phone number.
3. Complete identity verification: scan your passport and do the face check the app asks for.
4. Add your foreign card and let the app verify it.

Verification can take a few minutes to a day. You do not want to be doing this while a taxi driver waits, so finish it early.

## Binding a foreign card, step by step

Menu labels shift between app versions, so treat these as the shape of the flow, not exact button text (not yet verified — check in-app).

- Open the wallet or "Me" section.
- Find "Bank Cards" or "Add a card."
- Enter your card number, expiry, and CVC.
- Enter the billing name and address exactly as your bank has them.
- Confirm the SMS or bank-app code your card issuer sends.

Both apps support Visa, Mastercard, American Express, JCB, Discover, Diners Club, and UnionPay International. All seven are current: Amex only joined Alipay in February 2025, and UnionPay International was added to both apps in November 2024. Confirm your specific card in-app anyway — support is by card, not just by network. Tell your bank you will use the card in China so it does not block the first charge as suspicious.

## Fees and limits to expect

Expect a fee on larger payments. Per Tenpay's own terms (Tenpay is the entity behind WeChat Pay): no fee on single payments of ¥200 or under, and a 3% fee on the **full amount** of any single payment above ¥200 — not just the part over ¥200. Refunds return the fee proportionally.

WeChat Pay currently waives that fee for new international-card users: 90 consecutive days from first binding, on daily spending up to ¥1,000, running until 31 December 2026. This is a WeChat-only promotion — Alipay has no equivalent offer. Check the current terms in-app before relying on it.

Caps come in two tiers. On the basic tier: about ¥6,500 per payment, ¥50,000 per month, ¥65,000 per year. After completing the deeper identity verification: roughly ¥35,000 per payment and ¥350,000 per year. Sources that quote "¥50,000 a year" appear to be reading WeChat's **monthly** ceiling as an annual one. For normal travel spending — meals, taxis, tickets, shopping — you are very unlikely to hit any of these.

Your card issuer may add its own foreign-transaction fee on top. Check that with your bank (not yet verified — depends on your card).

## When it won't work — carry backup cash

Mobile pay covers most of China, but not all of it. Plan for the gaps:

- Small stalls, rural vendors, and some street food take only domestic-linked QR codes.
- A card can be declined on the first try; a retry or the other app often works.
- App or network trouble happens.

Carry a few hundred yuan in cash as backup, keep both apps installed, and you will get through the day. For a longer stay, withdrawing cash from an ATM with your foreign card is a reliable fallback.
