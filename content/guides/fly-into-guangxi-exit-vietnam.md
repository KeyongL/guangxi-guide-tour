The route: fly into Nanning 南宁 or Beihai 北海 visa-free and ride the coast for up to 10 days, ending at the Dongxing 东兴 border on the Beilun River 北仑河, with Móng Cái, Vietnam on the far bank. **How you leave from there depends on which visa program you are on.**

If your passport is on China's 30-day unilateral visa-free list (~48 countries, mostly Europe plus Australia and New Zealand; the USA is not on it), you cross at Dongxing and carry on into Vietnam. If you are using the 240-hour (10-day) transit visa-free policy (~54 nationalities, including the USA), plan to fly out from your entry airport instead: transit status is tied to a fixed list of designated entry and exit ports, and we have not confirmed that the Dongxing land port is on it (not yet verified). Confirm with China's National Immigration Administration (NIA) for your nationality before booking. Flying out stays inside the clearly written rules.

## Which visa program you are on

- **30-day visa-free** (~48 countries): 30 days, no onward-ticket requirement, no restriction on leaving by land. The Dongxing exit is routine for you.
- **240-hour transit** (~54 nationalities): max 10 days, only as a genuine A-to-C transit — arrive from one country, hold a confirmed ticket onward to a different one. Vietnam is your country C; a Hanoi-bound bus or flight booking satisfies the onward-travel check.

Both lists change; check the current NIA lists before booking.

## Entry: Nanning or Beihai

Both are designated 240-hour entry ports:

- **Nanning Wuxu International Airport 南宁吴圩国际机场 (NNG)** — Guangxi's main hub. Direct flights from Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, Singapore, Hanoi, and Hong Kong. There is no metro line to the airport — take airport shuttle bus Line 4 (¥20, boards at Terminal 2 Gate 6, ~60–70 min into the city) or the airport's own high-speed rail (EMU) station (~18–36 min to Nanning East 南宁东站).
- **Beihai Fucheng Airport 北海福成机场 (BHY)** — mostly domestic flights; only useful with an international connection, which complicates transit status. Enter and exit China once.

At immigration, have ready: passport, arrival card, onward booking to Vietnam (app screenshot is fine), and your first night's hotel address. Transit travelers use the dedicated 240-hour/TWOV counter.

## Nanning to Beihai by train

Around 58 high-speed trains run daily from Nanning East 南宁东 to Beihai 北海, 06:48 to 22:29, taking 56 minutes to 1 h 40 min for the 197 km. Second class costs ¥87, first class ¥140.

Buying tickets: book on **Trip.com** (English, international cards; the ticket links to your passport — no paper ticket, scan the passport at the gate) or the official **China Railway 12306 app** (English version; registration needs passport verification and can take a day — set it up before you fly). Same-day booking usually works on this route; holiday weeks sell out. Arrive 30–40 minutes early for security and ID checks.

## Beihai and Weizhou 涠洲岛

Beihai fills 2–3 days: Silver Beach 银滩, the colonial-era Old Street 老街, and Weizhou Island. Ferries leave from **Beihai International Passenger Port 北海国际客运港** to Xijiao Pier 西角码头 on Weizhou; crossing takes roughly 80–110 minutes and one-way fares run about ¥150. Book on the Laiyou 来游吧 platform, the official Beibu Gulf ferry channel, or Trip.com; you need your passport, and island entry includes a fixed ¥98 scenic-area fee. July–August is typhoon season on this route and sailings get cancelled in rough weather with little notice — check the ferry the same week you travel, since a cancelled sailing can eat a transit day you cannot spare.

## Beihai to Dongxing 东兴

Direct buses run from Beihai's long-distance bus station to Dongxing, roughly 2.5–3 hours, ¥65, departing every 30–90 minutes; buy at the counter with your passport. Alternative: high-speed train Beihai → Fangchenggang North 防城港北 (~1 h 20 min, ¥49 second class / ¥78 first class), then a connecting bus from outside the station to Dongxing bus station — roughly 50 km, about an hour, ¥15–20. You do not need to detour through Fangchenggang city to pick it up. Departures look timed around train arrivals rather than running on a fixed frequent schedule (not yet verified), so leave a buffer instead of counting on an immediate connection. A taxi covers the same leg in about an hour; we have not found a reliable fare for it. Sleep in Dongxing the night before crossing — pick any hotel within walking distance of the border gate, and confirm "accepts foreign guests" when booking on Trip.com.

Rough daily cost on this route: budget ¥300–450 per day for a mid-range hotel, trains, and meals — this holds for the mainland leg. Weizhou Island accommodation roughly doubles in the June–August peak season (which includes right now), so budget more for those specific nights.

## The crossing at Dongxing

The border is the Beilun River; Móng Cái sits on the far bank. Ordinary passport holders cross at the **Bắc Luân II bridge** — a vehicle bridge with no footpath, so you do not walk across it; a shuttle bus carries pedestrians the short distance between the Chinese and Vietnamese processing halls. The older Bắc Luân I bridge is now restricted to border-resident permit and multi-entry permit holders and visa-on-arrival travellers — crossing setups here have shifted before, so it's still worth confirming locally the day before. Gate hours are 08:00–21:00 China time / 07:00–20:00 Vietnam time, official policy since March 2026 (no longer a trial). Typical wait is around 50 minutes. Exit China first, then clear Vietnamese entry on the far side. From Móng Cái bus station, buses continue to Hạ Long (~3 h) and on to Hà Nội (~4–4.5 h). Arrange your Vietnam visa or e-visa in advance — there is no visa on arrival at this land border.

Two hard rules:

- **⚠️ 240-hour transit travelers: plan to fly out, not walk out.** Transit status is tied to a fixed list of designated entry and exit ports. We have not confirmed that the Dongxing land port is on that list, and the working assumption here is that it is not (not yet verified). Treat Dongxing as a day trip to the border and fly out from your entry airport. Ask the NIA directly for your nationality before you commit. 30-day visa-free nationals and visa holders are unaffected — they can cross.
- **⚠️ Do not route via Friendship Pass 友谊关.** That crossing is in Chongzuo 崇左, outside the area 240-hour transit lets you travel in. Note the difference between the two rules: being inside the transit *area* (Dongxing is; Friendship Pass is not) is not the same as being a designated *port* you may exit through. Dongxing clears the first test and, as above, we cannot confirm it clears the second.

## Confirm before you book

- Your list: 240-hour transit or 30-day visa-free (current NIA lists).
- Transit travelers: NIA confirmation of the Dongxing overland exit.
- Onward booking to Vietnam in hand for transit entry.
- Ferry sailings for your Weizhou dates, especially in typhoon season (Jul–Aug) — schedules and cancellations shift week to week.
- Vietnam e-visa approved before you reach the border.
