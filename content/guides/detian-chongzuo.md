Check your entry status before you plan this trip. Detian Waterfall 德天瀑布 and Friendship Pass 友谊关 are in Chongzuo 崇左, a border prefecture that is **not** covered by China's 240-hour transit visa-free policy. If you entered China under the transit rule, you cannot legally travel here. This corner of Guangxi is open only to people who need no visa under the separate 30-day visa-free program, or who hold a valid Chinese visa. Sort your paperwork first. The scenery is not worth an immigration problem.

## Why your entry status matters here

The 240-hour (10-day) transit visa-free policy is tied to a fixed list of ports and administrative areas. Chongzuo 崇左 is outside that zone. A transit-visa-free traveler who comes here is out of bounds, full stop.

Two groups can visit legally:

- Nationals of the 30-day visa-free program. As of 2026 this covers roughly 48 countries, mostly in Europe, plus Australia and New Zealand. The United States is **not** on this list.
- Anyone holding a valid Chinese visa (tourist, business, or other).

If you are not sure which group you fall into, confirm it with a Chinese embassy or consulate before booking anything. Do not guess.

## Getting to the falls from Nanning 南宁

Detian 德天瀑布 sits in Daxin 大新 county, about 230 km from Nanning 南宁. Plan on a half-day each way.

- Direct tourist buses run from Nanning 南宁 to the Detian scenic area. Departures reported at 08:30 and 11:30 from Langdong station, fare around ¥75, journey 2.5 to 3 hours (not yet verified).
- The last stretch runs right along the China–Vietnam border. Keep your passport and entry documents on you; this is a border zone and you may be checked.

Buses back to Nanning are limited and fill up. Check the return time the moment you arrive, or book a same-day round trip.

## At Detian 德天瀑布: the falls and the raft

Detian Transnational Waterfall 德天瀑布 is one of the largest transnational waterfalls in Asia. It straddles the China–Vietnam border: the Chinese side is Detian, the Vietnamese side is Ban Gioc. The falls run widest and loudest in the wet season, roughly May to September.

- Entrance ticket, including the in-park shuttle bus: around ¥115 by day; night tickets reported at ¥168 to ¥198 (not yet verified).
- Bamboo-raft ride to the base of the falls: around ¥30 to ¥48 per person for a 10 to 15-minute ride (not yet verified).

The raft is the reason to come. It takes you close to the churn at the foot of the falls, spray and all. It passes the boundary markers between the two countries.

The border runs through the pools here. Stay on marked paths and viewing decks. Do not step across the boundary line or onto the Vietnamese side. It is an international frontier, not a photo backdrop.

## Friendship Pass 友谊关, Pingxiang 凭祥

Friendship Pass 友谊关, at Pingxiang 凭祥 about two hours south, is the historic main overland gate between China and Vietnam. On the Vietnamese side it opens toward Lạng Sơn.

For travelers with the correct entry status and documents, this is a normal, legal border crossing at an official port with standard immigration. That is the only way to cross here: through the checkpoint, passport in hand, stamps in order.

The pass itself is also a sight. The old gate tower and its history draw visitors who never intend to cross. Treat the area as what it is: an active, staffed border post. Follow posted signs and the instructions of officials.

## Plan around the visa rule

One more time, because it is the whole point: this region is non-twov. The transit visa-free policy does not reach Chongzuo 崇左.

Before you commit:

1. Confirm your entry status. 30-day visa-free national, or valid Chinese visa. Nothing else works here.
2. If you are on a 240-hour transit entry, drop this trip. Stay inside the permitted zone.
3. Carry your passport and entry documents the whole day. This is a border area with checks.
4. Lock in your return transport early.

Get the paperwork right and Detian 德天瀑布 is one of the best day trips in Guangxi. Get it wrong and you should not be here at all.
