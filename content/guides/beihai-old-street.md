Beihai Old Street 北海老街 is a free, flat, roughly 1.3 km strip of treaty-port arcade houses on Zhuhai Road 珠海路. Walking it takes 20–30 minutes; with food stops and photos, budget 1–2 hours. It sits inside the 240-hour transit visa-free zone, so no extra paperwork. Verdict: worth 1–2 hours as an add-on to Silver Beach 银滩 or a Weizhou ferry day — not worth a special trip on its own.

## Getting there

- **From Silver Beach 银滩:** Bus 3 (¥2, board at the Yintan 银滩 stop) runs about 10.7 km to Beibu Gulf Square 北部湾广场; from there walk roughly 1 km (about 15 min) to the street. A tourist "Chang You Beihai" A-line bus also links Silver Beach and the Old Street directly (frequency and fare not yet verified). A ride-hail car takes about 15–25 minutes and runs around ¥25.
- **From the Weizhou ferry port (Beihai International Ferry Terminal 北海国际客运港):** Bus 3 stops at the terminal and continues toward the city center — get off at Beibu Gulf Square 北部湾广场 and walk the same 1 km. A taxi takes about 15 minutes (not yet verified — no direct data for this route; it is shorter than the Silver Beach run).
- Buses 2 and 25 stop closer: both have a stop named Beihai Old Street 北海老街, so the walk from stop to arcades is short, though we have not measured it (not yet verified).

## The walk itself

The street runs east–west along the old waterfront and is one straight line — no route to plan. Start from whichever end you reach first; the densest restored stretch is in the middle. The pavement is old and uneven; arcades narrow where stalls spill out.

The buildings are qilou 骑楼: upper floors ride out over the sidewalk on pillars, making a shaded walkway against sun and rain. Beihai opened as a treaty port after the 1876 Sino-British Treaty of Yantai, and consulates, churches, and trading houses followed. Most facades date from the late 1800s to the 1920s: two to three storeys, arched windows, carved lintels. Look up — the upper floors are the point, and shopfront-level walking misses them. The street is 1.27 km long — the figure in the Ministry of Culture and Tourism's 2023 listing of it as a national tourism and leisure block. The official wording is more specific than the usual retelling: it is billed as the longest *straight* and best-preserved arcade street in Lingnan, not simply the longest in southern China. A 1.44 km figure circulates too, with no official source behind it.

## Free vs ticketed

The street itself is free, always open, no gate. Four marked buildings — former consulates and trading houses — hold small museums: the History and Culture Museum ¥60, the Wood Carving Museum ¥30, the Nixing Pottery Museum ¥30, and the Watercolour Museum ¥30, or ¥100 for all four. They open 08:30–12:30 and 15:00–18:00. (One source disagrees on the History and Culture Museum price, so check at the door.) Shops and food stalls run roughly 08:00–22:00, with bars and cafes open later.

## What to eat

Prices below are rough street-stall levels:

- **Beihai fen 北海粉** — not one dish but the catch-all name for the local rice-noodle bowls: beef brisket 牛腩粉, crab 蟹仔粉, seafood 海鲜粉 and others. Reckon on ¥8–20, and 2026 prices cluster at the ¥15–20 end rather than the bottom.
- **Jian dui 煎堆** — deep-fried sesame balls, a few yuan apiece (not yet verified — no local price source found; figures online are for the Guangdong and Hainan versions).
- **Seafood skewers and grilled shellfish** — sold from small stalls; agree the price before they cook.

Coffee shops line the middle stretch if you want to sit.

## Pearl-shop warning

Beihai markets itself as a pearl town, and the street is thick with pearl sellers targeting tourists. Marked prices are opening bids, not prices; quality grading is opaque to non-experts. Do not buy loose pearls or "bargains" here unless you can grade pearls yourself.

## When to go

Go before 10:00 or after 17:00. Midday brings tour groups, heat, and the worst light for the facades. Evening adds open bars and snack stalls.

## Pair it with

- **Silver Beach 银滩** — one bus 3 ride away; do the street in the morning, the beach after.
- **Weizhou Island 涠洲岛** — the ferry terminal is on the same bus 3 line, so the street slots into an arrival or departure day. Ferry times and tickets: see the [Weizhou Island guide](/guides/weizhou-island-complete-guide).
